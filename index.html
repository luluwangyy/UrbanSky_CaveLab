The illumination in dense urban environments can vary dramatically due to shadows and reflections from nearby buildings. Many urban locations never even see the sun—they only capture indirect illumination from nearby infrastructure. The goal of UrbanSky is to capture this diversity of real-world lighting environments in New York City. UrbanSky is a large dataset of XXX high dynamic range (HDR) lighting environments captured across New York City. At each location, we used a 360° camera to capture an HDR panorama and a pyranometer to measure the global horizontal irradiance (see Acquisition for more information). Alongside each capture, we include metadata that specifies the date, time of day, GPS location, and current weather conditions. UrbanSky is publicly available to the research community to encourage future work in solar energy and computer graphics.


We have released version 1.0 of the dataset below, which contains XXX HDR lighting environments. We continue to capture data and will release a new version of UrbanSky as the dataset grows.
